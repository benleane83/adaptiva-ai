## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**LLM assessment:** The site does not look like generic AI output. The navy-and-gold wave, serif/sans pairing, restrained voice, real founder imagery, and specific Copilot focus give it a credible identity. The risk is template drift rather than AI slop: repeated rounded page-hero panels, repeated navy CTA panels, pill buttons, and card grids flatten the distinctive original mockup into familiar B2B grammar.

**Deterministic scan:** The CLI returned 9 warnings across the eight HTML pages: eight `single-font` warnings and one `marketing-buzzword` warning in `copilot-adoption-enablement.html` around “Enablement / Empower your people.” All eight font warnings are false positives because computed styles confirm Cormorant Garamond headings and Montserrat body text. The buzzword warning is credible but low severity.

**Visual overlays:** Mutable injection succeeded in a fresh browser context on Home, Solutions, Industries, Contact, and Resources. Overlay totals ranged from 6 to 11 per page and highlighted low contrast, justified text, viewport-edge text, border-plus-wide-shadow combinations, nested cards, and dark-mode/glow patterns. The dark-mode warning on Resources is a false positive; several border/shadow and nested-container findings are credible. Browser resizing was capped at effective widths of 960px and 260px, so the severe clipping observed at 260px is a stress signal, not evidence of failure at the requested 390px viewport.

## Overall Impression

Adaptiva AI already feels more trustworthy than the average AI consultancy site. Its biggest opportunity is to become as specific and visually committed as its new brief: let the original wave composition dominate the first viewport, then immediately prove what Adaptiva does, for whom, and why it is credible. Today the homepage moves from a strong promise directly to another CTA, leaving the middle of the buying argument missing.

## What's Working

1. **Ownable identity:** The adaptive wave, navy/gold palette, and contrasting type families form a recognizable visual system.
2. **Human voice:** Copy emphasizes readiness, confidence, work, and adoption rather than technology spectacle.
3. **Sound foundation:** Semantic landmarks, skip links, responsive navigation, dialog elements, real imagery, and shared tokens provide a solid implementation base.

## Priority Issues

### P1: The homepage underuses the original mockup's strongest composition

**Why it matters:** The wave is the most distinctive brand asset, but the white veil, moderate headline scale, pill actions, and added CTA section reduce its cinematic navy-and-gold depth. The result is polished but less memorable than the approved inspiration.

**Fix:** Recompose the first viewport as one deliberate scene: preserve more navy in the lower/right wave, raise the desktop hero scale, keep the promise to two lines, use mockup-inspired rectangular hero actions with arrow/play icons, and restore a gold active-navigation underline. Validate image crop and text contrast at desktop, tablet, and mobile before settling overlay stops.

**Suggested command:** `$impeccable bolder index.html hero`

### P1: The homepage skips the evidence and offer layer

**Why it matters:** After “AI that Adapts,” visitors see another generic next-step panel. Decision-makers still do not know the concrete engagement, deliverables, timeframe, or evidence; practitioners do not see a lower-commitment path.

**Fix:** Replace the current standalone CTA panel with a compact narrative sequence: two clear solution pathways, a human proof block using founder/customer evidence that can be substantiated, a simple “how we work” progression, then one consultation CTA plus one lower-commitment resource or complimentary-session action. Do not invent metrics or logos.

**Suggested command:** `$impeccable shape homepage buyer journey and proof`

### P1: Keyboard focus and form recovery are not visibly designed

**Why it matters:** Inner inputs explicitly remove their outline, but no `:focus-within` treatment is defined on the control wrapper. Browser inspection found no visible focus treatment on the first tested keyboard target. This undermines the brief's accessibility commitment and makes the highest-stakes action less trustworthy.

**Fix:** Add consistent `:focus-visible` styles for links/buttons and `:focus-within` styles for form wrappers, using a high-contrast navy/gold ring. Add field-level error associations and verify success, failure, loading, and retry states with keyboard-only testing.

**Suggested command:** `$impeccable audit accessibility and contact states`

### P2: Inner pages repeat a generic rounded-panel template

**Why it matters:** About, Solutions, Industries, Resources, Contact, and both solution pages begin with the same rounded gradient panel, while most end with the same navy CTA panel. Consistency becomes sameness, and the signature brand asset largely disappears after the homepage.

**Fix:** Create two or three purposeful inner-page compositions rather than one universal frame: founder-led editorial imagery on About, immersive solution artwork with structured deliverables on solution pages, and a practical evidence/table treatment for Industries and Resources. Use subtle wave crops or gold contours as connective tissue, not a background pasted everywhere.

**Suggested command:** `$impeccable layout inner page system`

### P2: The consultation form asks too much before trust is earned

**Why it matters:** Seven required fields appear at once inside a callout nested in a page-hero panel. Placeholder-led labels and icon-heavy controls increase visual density, especially on narrow screens, and procurement/practitioner visitors have no clear expectation of what happens after submission.

**Fix:** Ask only for name, work email, organisation, and a short priority initially; make phone and role optional or collect them later. Keep persistent visible labels, state response time/privacy expectations, remove unnecessary nested framing, and preserve the complimentary session as a distinct lower-commitment path.

**Suggested command:** `$impeccable distill contact.html`

## Persona Red Flags

**Morgan, AI or business decision-maker:** The homepage promise resonates, but Morgan cannot evaluate delivery model, proof, expected outcomes, or engagement scope before being asked to book. The jump from hero to CTA feels premature.

**Priya, programme owner or practitioner:** “Discover Your AI Journey” does not tell Priya whether she will receive an assessment, roadmap, workshop, training, or reusable material. Nearly every route leads to a sales conversation rather than a practical starting resource.

**Jordan, keyboard and mobile user:** Jordan encounters controls without a reliable visible focus state. At the browser's 260px stress width, the open mobile menu and nested page structures heavily constrain content; exact 390px validation remains required.

## Minor Observations

- Repeated tracked eyebrows appear across several pages despite the new brief reserving them for meaningful metadata.
- `feature-list` and `template-note` still use a 3px gold side stripe, now explicitly marked as migration debt in `DESIGN.md`.
- Cards and page panels commonly combine a hairline border with a wide soft shadow.
- The Digital Content Creation page is mostly prose and unordered lists despite being the service where visual examples should carry the argument.
- Resources presents unavailable samples with disabled elements that resemble active “Watch Sample” links; label them “Coming soon” with clearly non-interactive styling.
- The site mixes US and UK spelling (`organization` / `organisation`, `centered` / `centred`); standardize visible copy to the chosen voice.

## Questions to Consider

- Is the homepage meant to qualify visitors directly for consulting, or should it first let them understand Adaptiva through a useful resource or complimentary session?
- What existing evidence can be published now: founder credentials, named customer work, testimonials, sample deliverables, or anonymized outcomes?
- Should the original mockup's rectangular action geometry become a hero-only signature, while pills remain for compact navigation and utility actions?
- Which two inner pages deserve distinct art direction first: About for trust, or Copilot Adoption for commercial clarity?
